Designed by Thomas D'Astolto

My Neighbor Totoro

Totoro’s famous umbrella scene was inspired by Hayao Miyazaki’s childhood memory of waiting for a bus with his mother. The sound of the raindrops bouncing off Totoro’s umbrella was created using a water tank and tiny objects to mimic the unique sound!

Howl's Moving Castle

Hayao Miyazaki based Howl’s dramatic personality on a close friend of his who had a flair for the theatrical. The infamous scene where Howl sulks and declares, “I see no point in living if I can’t be beautiful,” was inspired by that friend’s real-life antics!

Castle in the Sky

The language of Laputa, used on the floating island, was designed to resemble Sanskrit. Hayao Miyazaki wanted the language to feel ancient and universal, paying homage to humanity’s shared cultural heritage.

Princess Momonoke

The kodama (tree spirits) in Princess Mononoke are based on a Japanese folklore belief that spirits reside in trees. Their rattling sound was created using wooden instruments and was meant to evoke a mysterious yet playful tone, making them both eerie and endearing!
